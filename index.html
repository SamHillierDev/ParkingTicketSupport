Understanding PCN Appeals

A Penalty Charge Notice (PCN) is a parking fine issued by local authorities or Transport for London. If you believe your PCN was issued incorrectly, you have the right to appeal.

Important: You typically have 28 days from the date of issue to challenge a PCN. If you pay within 14 days, you may receive a 50% discount.

Grounds for Appeal

You can appeal a PCN if:

  • The parking restrictions were not clearly signed or marked
  • You were not the owner of the vehicle at the time
  • The vehicle was stolen or taken without your consent
  • You had a valid permit or exemption
  • There was a medical emergency or breakdown
  • The PCN was issued incorrectly (wrong vehicle, location, or time)
  • You were loading or unloading goods
  • The parking meter or pay-and-display machine was faulty

How to Appeal

Step 1: Informal Challenge

Within 14 days of receiving the PCN, you can make an informal challenge. This is usually done online through the local authority's website or by post. Include:

  • Your PCN reference number
  • Clear explanation of why you believe the PCN is incorrect
  • Any supporting evidence (photos, documents, witness statements)

Step 2: Formal Representation

If your informal challenge is rejected, you'll receive a Notice to Owner. You then have 28 days to make a formal representation. This must be done in writing and should include:

  • All relevant evidence
  • Reference to specific regulations or laws
  • Clear arguments supporting your case

Step 3: Appeal to an Adjudicator

If your formal representation is rejected, you can appeal to an independent adjudicator at a tribunal. This is free and the decision is binding on both parties.

Tip: Always keep copies of all correspondence and evidence. Take photos of parking signs, road markings, and any relevant circumstances at the time of the alleged contravention.

Evidence to Collect

  • Photographs of parking signs and road markings
  • Photos of your vehicle's position
  • Receipts or proof of payment if applicable
  • Witness statements
  • Medical certificates if relevant
  • Breakdown recovery documentation if applicable
  • Any correspondence with the authority

Time Limits

  • 14 days: Pay at reduced rate (50% discount) or make informal challenge
  • 28 days: Pay full amount or make formal representation after receiving Notice to Owner
  • 28 days: Appeal to adjudicator after formal representation is rejected

Useful Resources

For more information, visit:

  • Your local authority's website for specific PCN appeal procedures
  • Transport for London (if applicable) - tfl.gov.uk
  • Traffic Penalty Tribunal - trafficpenaltytribunal.gov.uk
Remember: Each case is unique. If you're unsure about your appeal, consider seeking advice from a qualified professional or citizens advice service.
